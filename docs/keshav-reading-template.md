# Keshav Reading Template

Status: local review template.

Round: 304.

This template adapts the Keshav three-pass reading idea into a conservative
TuringResearch Scholar workflow.

## Pass 1: Bird's-Eye

Goal: decide whether the paper deserves deeper attention.

Checklist:

- Read title, abstract, introduction, section headings, figures, and conclusion.
- Identify broad category and context.
- Record obvious relevance and missing evidence.
- Do not claim complete paper reading.

## Pass 2: Content Grasp

Goal: understand the main method and what should enter a review scaffold.

Checklist:

- Read method and experiment sections closely.
- Draft inputs, outputs, representations, and method mechanics.
- Record borrow / not-copy notes.
- Identify references or citations that need review.

## Pass 3: Deep Understanding

Goal: reconstruct assumptions, failure modes, and downstream risks.

Checklist:

- Rebuild the method in your own words.
- Check figures, equations, and experiment tables.
- Map to method-card fields.
- Update collision and related-work questions only as review notes.

## Five Cs

- Category: what kind of paper this appears to be.
- Context: where it fits relative to the project.
- Correctness: whether claims are supported by inspected evidence.
- Contributions: what the paper may contribute.
- Clarity: what remains unclear.

## Boundary

- no final paper conclusion;
- no camera-ready paper text;
- no verified citation claim from fake/demo notes;
- requires human review.
