## Rationale

The workspace layer must land before cross-project graphs, privacy gates, and
plugin metadata can be meaningful. Template work follows because every future
project needs a stable structure. Cross-project evidence and privacy policy
then define how projects interact safely.

Plugin, MCP, capability, and skill marketplace work should come after the
workspace surfaces are stable, because those systems describe and expose
capabilities. Paper writing and benchmark replay should come later because they
depend on stable project and evidence conventions.

## Dependency Rules

- Do not expose plugin or marketplace surfaces before capability manifests and
  safety levels exist.
- Do not create cross-project claims until privacy and human review gates are
  explicit.
- Do not generate paper prose until evidence and citation gates are available.
- Do not treat benchmark replay as a real experiment result.
