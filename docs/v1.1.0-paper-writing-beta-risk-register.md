# v1.1.0 Paper Writing Beta Risk Register

Status: active.

Round: 221.

## Risk Summary

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Draft text is mistaken for final paper prose | High | Label all outputs beta/review-only and require human review |
| Missing results are hidden by fluent prose | High | Keep missing result table guard mandatory |
| Fake/demo evidence is promoted to observed evidence | High | Preserve evidence status and block observed fake statuses |
| Unsupported claims slip into paper sections | High | Reuse unsafe claim guard and assembly gate |
| Citation fixtures are treated as real citations | High | Keep citation status guard and human review fields |
| Advisor notes become final claims | Medium | Map advisor notes to paper actions, not final text |
| Live adapters are accidentally required | Medium | Keep live adapters opt-in and out of beta scope |
| Private paths enter paper outputs | High | Run privacy/name/security checks on beta fixtures |

## Claim Risks

The beta must block unsupported claims such as:

- completed VGGT experiment success;
- SparseConv3D success;
- quantitative improvements without evidence;
- completed citation-grade review when only fixtures exist;
- final paper readiness.

## Evidence Risks

Every paragraph candidate must retain evidence references and evidence status.
If evidence is missing, the output should show the gap instead of smoothing it
over.

## Citation Risks

Citation candidates may appear as planning items, but only reviewed sources can
be used as citation-grade support. Fixture-derived notes remain non-citation
review aids.

## Release Risks

The beta should not block v1.1 unless the wording overclaims or tests show
that missing results, unsafe claims, or fake/demo evidence can be promoted.
