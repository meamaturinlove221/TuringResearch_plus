## Why This Matters

The v1.3 release story is operational discipline: make the original parity path
clearer, safer, and more runnable. Adding ARIS runtime concepts now would blur
scope, increase safety review burden, and risk turning a stable parity release
into a new agent-runtime experiment.

## Allowed ARIS Use In v1.3

Allowed:

- reference-only discussion;
- study roadmap;
- risk matrix;
- future backlog;
- small fake prototype planning after a separate scope lock.

Not allowed:

- production runtime;
- automatic review loop;
- proof or correctness claims;
- autonomous paper-claim audit;
- session lifecycle hooks with side effects;
- automated research loop.

## Binding Wording

Use:

- `future reference`;
- `deferred`;
- `study only`;
- `not v1.3 implementation`;
- `requires design and safety review`.

Do not use:

- `implemented`;
- `enabled`;
- `production`;
- `runtime available`;
- `automatic`;
- `replaces human review`.
