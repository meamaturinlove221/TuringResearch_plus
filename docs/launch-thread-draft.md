## Thread

1. I have been building TuringResearch Plus: a local-first Research OS for
   evidence-led research workflows.

2. The problem: research state gets scattered across papers, artifacts, logs,
   notes, dashboards, advisor feedback, and half-written claims.

3. TuringResearch Plus connects evidence ledgers, artifact audits, route DSL,
   paper review, advisor packs, dashboards, plugin manifests, privacy gates,
   and replay checks.

4. It is deliberately review-first. Fake/demo workflows run locally by default.
   Live adapters are optional. Unknown plugins are not executed.

5. The most important rule: planned work is not observed evidence. Missing
   evidence stays visible.

6. It includes public demos, static dashboards, paper scaffolds, plugin safety
   docs, and split dry-run examples for future case/example repos.

7. I also dogfooded the workflow on a VGGT human-prior case. The case shows
   evidence management and route discipline, not final experiment success.

8. What I am proud of: the system refuses to overclaim. It is built around
   safety gates, contracts, tests, and human review.

9. v1.0 direction: stable core API, public demo, plugin manifest stability,
   release candidate hardening, and optional case/examples split repos.

10. If you care about research tooling, local-first workflows, or honest AI
    engineering, the repo is worth a look.
