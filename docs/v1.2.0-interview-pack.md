# v1.2.0 Interview Pack

Status: portfolio refresh.

Round: 253.

This pack explains the v1.2 engineering story for interviews: TuringResearch
first closed stable original-reference parity, then deferred ARIS-style
research loops until the base system was safer, clearer, and more testable.

It does not add functionality, claim research success, or turn review aids into
automatic research.

## One-line Positioning

TuringResearch is a local-first Research OS that turns research work into
reviewable evidence, artifacts, routes, demos, dashboards, and safety gates.

## v1.2 Interview Thesis

The v1.2 decision was not to chase the newest agent ideas first. The stronger
engineering move was to stabilize parity with the original reference systems:

- session and pod context safety;
- scholar and web pipeline usability;
- MCP fake/live configuration;
- skill SOP routing;
- campaign, vault, ontology, stress-test, and experiment-runbook workflows;
- public demo and full fake replay gates.

ARIS remains valuable, but it was deliberately moved to v1.3 study planning.

## What To Show

1. Start with the Research OS architecture.
2. Show the Reference Parity Dashboard.
3. Explain original-reference parity first.
4. Explain why ARIS was deferred.
5. Show how tests, contracts, docs, and gates keep the system runnable.
6. Close with the fake/live, privacy, and no-overclaim boundaries.

## What This Demonstrates

- Scope discipline: parity before speculative expansion.
- Safety judgment: risky loops and automation remain deferred.
- Product clarity: public demos show capabilities without private data.
- Engineering maturity: contracts, tests, gates, and docs move together.
- Practical architecture: monorepo modules are strengthened before future
  splits or larger runtime ideas.

## What Not To Overclaim

- Do not say TuringResearch automates research.
- Do not say it writes final papers.
- Do not say it replaces advisors or human review.
- Do not say demos are live experiment results.
- Do not present ARIS features as v1.2 work.
- Do not say reference parity means code was copied from upstream.

## Recommended Interview Flow

Use the architecture story for the first two minutes, the parity story for the
technical depth section, and the ARIS deferral story for engineering judgment.
If the interviewer asks about ambition, explain that v1.3 is the right place to
study ARIS because v1.2 first makes the base system reliable.
