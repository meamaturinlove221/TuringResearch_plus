# ARIS vs TuringResearch Positioning

Status: positioning note.

Round: 233.

ARIS and TuringResearch are adjacent but not identical directions.

## TuringResearch Current Position

TuringResearch is a local-first Research OS focused on:

- evidence ledgers;
- artifact audits;
- route plans and hard gates;
- paper and citation review aids;
- advisor bundles;
- public-safe demos;
- privacy/security gates;
- human review.

It is not an autonomous research system and does not claim automatic experiment
success or final paper generation.

## ARIS As Future Reference

ARIS is valuable as a future reference for:

- cross-model review;
- claim audit;
- result-to-claim verification;
- experiment audit;
- proof-checking ideas;
- meta-optimization;
- session lifecycle hooks;
- paper resubmit workflows.

These ideas may be studied after v1.2 parity work.

## Why The Separation Matters

Directly merging ARIS into v1.2 would:

- raise stability risk;
- blur review aid versus automation;
- complicate safety gates;
- distract from Neocortica/yogsoth parity;
- risk overclaiming paper or experiment results;
- make the public story less honest.

## Positioning Rule

Use this language:

> ARIS is a future research reference for v1.3 study and possible v1.4 selective
> adoption. v1.2 remains focused on original reference parity and stable
> local-first workflows.

Do not say:

- ARIS features are implemented in v1.2;
- TuringResearch performs cross-model review today;
- TuringResearch proof-checks papers today;
- TuringResearch automatically audits final paper claims today;
- TuringResearch automatically optimizes research strategy today.
