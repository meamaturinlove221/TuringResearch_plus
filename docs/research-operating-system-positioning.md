# Research Operating System Positioning

Status: planning.

Round: 102.

TuringResearch Plus should be positioned as a research operating system for
evidence-backed local workflows. It coordinates the work around research rather
than replacing the researcher.

## Positioning Statement

TuringResearch Plus is a local-first research operating system that turns
research intent, papers, artifacts, experiments, failures, dashboards, and
advisor updates into typed, auditable workflows.

It helps a researcher answer:

- What are we trying to prove?
- What evidence exists?
- What is missing?
- Which artifacts support or weaken the claim?
- What should be read next?
- What should be run next?
- What can be safely shown to an advisor?
- What is ready for public release?

## What It Is

- A project workspace structure.
- An evidence and artifact organizer.
- A run ingest and dashboard layer.
- A related-work and paper review scaffold.
- An advisor communication builder.
- A plugin/MCP capability surface.
- A safety policy layer for local, live, and remote workflows.

## What It Is Not

- Not an automatic scientist.
- Not an automatic paper writer.
- Not a SaaS product by default.
- Not a remote execution controller.
- Not a source of verified experiment results.
- Not a substitute for human paper reading.
- Not a replacement for privacy, license, or advisor review.

## Core Loop

1. Define research intent.
2. Create or select a project template.
3. Gather papers, artifacts, and route specs.
4. Run fake/default workflows first.
5. Ingest real artifacts only through structured reports.
6. Audit evidence, artifacts, visuals, failures, and claims.
7. Build advisor-ready summaries.
8. Keep limitations visible.
9. Promote evidence only after human review.

## User-facing Promise

TuringResearch Plus should make research state legible. It should make weak
evidence obvious, missing artifacts visible, and advisor communication safer.
It should not hide uncertainty behind polished output.

## Public Positioning Guardrails

- Use "review-ready" and "evidence-backed" carefully.
- Do not imply automatic research discovery.
- Do not imply automatic experiment execution.
- Do not imply generated writing is publication-ready.
- Do not imply remote artifacts are verified.
- Keep fake/demo examples labeled as fake/demo.
