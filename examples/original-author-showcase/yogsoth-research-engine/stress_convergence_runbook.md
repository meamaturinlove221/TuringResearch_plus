## Summary

This academic workflow output turns research planning into a gated decision process. A proposed route should be stress-tested before implementation, compared against alternatives, and converted into a runbook only after risks, evidence gaps, and expected artifacts are clear.

## Stress Scenarios

A route should be tested against:

- missing evidence;
- unsupported claims;
- fake-result risk;
- artifact omission;
- weak citation support;
- privacy leakage;
- unsafe remote actions;
- route contradiction;
- advisor-report overclaim.

## Convergence Decision

A convergence report should compare candidates by:

- user value;
- research value;
- implementation feasibility;
- dependency readiness;
- risk;
- testability;
- release impact;
- failure recovery path.

## Experiment Runbook

A safe experiment runbook should specify:

- goal;
- non-goals;
- prerequisites;
- commands or manual steps;
- expected artifacts;
- hard gates;
- failure modes;
- cleanup;
- ingest requirements;
- report requirements.
