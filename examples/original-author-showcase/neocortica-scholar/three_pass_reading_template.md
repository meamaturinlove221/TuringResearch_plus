## Summary

This academic workflow output turns paper reading into a staged, auditable process. The value is not merely summarization, but preserving what was checked, what remains uncertain, and how the paper maps to an implementation or research route.

## Template

### Pass 1: Bird's-eye Scan

- What is the paper about?
- What problem does it claim to solve?
- What are the main contributions?
- Is it relevant enough for deeper reading?

### Pass 2: Content Understanding

- What are the method components?
- What are the inputs, outputs, and representations?
- What assumptions does the method rely on?
- What experiments support the claims?
- What limitations are visible?

### Pass 3: Deep Implementation Reading

- What would be required to reproduce the method?
- Which modules can be borrowed or adapted?
- Which claims require extra verification?
- What risks appear if applied to our project?
- What follow-up experiments should be planned?

## TuringResearch Demonstration

This template supports:

- paper reading reports;
- method cards;
- implementation gap analysis;
- related work positioning;
- experiment route planning;
- advisor-facing paper summaries.

## Safety Boundary

This is a reading workflow template, not a claim that a paper has been fully verified. Any final conclusion still requires source citations and human review.
